# Post-OJK Licensing, India Expansion

## After POJK Licensing (Path A)

Path A licensing (personalised investment advice) opens full product capability.

### What POJK Path A Enables

| Capability | Currently | After Path A |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Personalised advice | No (educational only) | Yes (licensed advisory) |
| Product recommendations | Illustrative only | Actual fund/product allocation |
| Transaction execution | No | Yes (through licensed platform) |
| User data retention | Stateless only | Persistent user accounts |
| Regulatory status | Path B (educational) | Full POJK 21/2011 licensed |

### Roadmap After Path A

**Month 1-3: Compliance and Technical Infrastructure**
- POJK 21/2011 application submission
- Compliance monitoring system (audit trail, disclosures)
- Licensed financial advisor onboarding (minimum 2 per product)
- Third-party risk assessment of fund products

**Month 4-6: Personalisation Layer**
- Replace illustrative allocations with actual product recommendations
- Integration with fund distribution APIs (Bareksa, Bibit, Ajaib)
- User accounts with KYC verification
- Personalised feasibility based on actual financial profile

**Month 7-12: Full Product Launch**
- Transaction execution (buy/sell funds within app)
- Performance tracking (actual vs projected)
- Periodic review reminders (annual goal check-in)
- Multi-device sync (mobile app)

## India Expansion

### Why India

- 1.4B population, large addressable market
- Growing middle class with similar financial planning needs
- English-language market (no translation burden)
- Regulatory pathway: SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India)
-仿范: Vestara's goal-first model doesn't exist in India

### India-Specific Adaptations Needed

**Regulatory:**
- SEBI registration for investment advisors
- ARN (AMFI Registration Number) for mutual fund distribution
- RBI guidelines for forex (education abroad goals involve USD/EUR)
- PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act) compliance

**Financial Products:**
- India instruments: PPF, NSC, EPF, mutual funds (different from ORI/SBR)
- Property costs: city-level data for Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Hyderabad
- Tax instruments: Section 80C/80D deductions are different from Indonesian tax

**UX:**
- Currency: INR instead of IDR
- Language: English (simpler) but Hindi support for mass market
- Financial literacy: lower than Indonesia (more education required)

### India MVP Scope

1. **Goal types:** Retirement, Children's Education, Property (same 3 core goals)
2. **Instruments:** PPF, NPS, mutual funds (equity + debt)
3. **Return assumptions:** Indian long-run averages (equity: 12-14%, debt: 7-8%)
4. **Regulatory:** SEBI investment advisor registration

### India Timeline Estimate

| Phase | Duration | Cost |
|-------|----------|------|
| Regulatory application | 6-12 months | Legal fees + SEBI fees |
| India financial model | 3 months | Data acquisition + model building |
| Platform localisation | 2 months | UX + currency + language |
| Beta launch | 1 month | India-specific testing |
| Full launch | Ongoing | India team + compliance |

**Honest assessment:** India expansion is a 2-3 year project after Indonesia is established. The regulatory timeline alone is 6-12 months for SEBI registration. Don't plan for India before Indonesia is profitable.
